Budget 2012

  • by:Sanjay Durgan
  • date:March 31, 2012
  • 3 Comments

The three primary concerns for this year’s budget were putting India back on the growth trajectory, fiscal consolidation and moderating inflation. Given the compulsions of governance, the budget did make an attempt to meet these objectives though doubts remain on the government’s ability to meet its target. A sense of general disappointment, though there was a little of everything for everyone.

The budget provided a comprehensive incentive package for the power sector at large and a slew of measures to kick start the reform process in the infrastructure sector. Government doubles the limit for Infrastructure Tax Free Bonds to Rs 60,000 crore.

  • The fiscal deficit for 2011‐12 is pegged at 5.9% and as per market estimates it may not be much lower in 2012‐13. Concerns on the fiscal front would continue to plague the economy thus inhibiting RBI from adopting aggressive rate cuts to foster the much needed investment cycle to accelerate growth.

  • After having grown at 8.4% in the preceding two years GDP growth for FY11-12 is expected at 6.9%, deceleration in industrial growth being the primary reason for the slowing growth. FY12-13 GDP growth is now projected at 7.6% (+/- 0.25%) and Fiscal Deficit at 5.1% of GDP.

  • Government’s endeavour is to keep central subsidies under 2% of GDP for FY12-13 and to bring it further down to 1.75% of the GDP over the next 3 years. No-win situation for the govt.

  • Current account deficit at 3.6% of GDP for FY11-12 continues to put pressure on the rupee

    • With too many variables at play it becomes difficult to project any clear trend going forward. Surging import bills can play the spoil sport to any initiatives towards controlling inflation and rationalizing subsidies.

    Markets have run up around 15% since the start of the year and a lot of expectations were being built into the macro-economic policy statement. Some consolidation in the markets can be expected as we go forward.
